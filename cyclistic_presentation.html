Executive Summary: The Core Divide

Analysis of ~3.8M trips confirms Casual Riders take **1.8x longer trips**.

The majority of trips are by Members (63.2%), but the largest conversion opportunity lies with the highly engaged, seasonal group of Casual Riders (36.8%).

Slide 2/7

Insight: Seasonal Growth & Weekend Focus

Casual ridership grows a massive **13x from Jan to Aug**.

With **38.3% of casual trips on weekends**, the Summer season is the high-impact conversion window. Campaigns must be leisure-focused.

Slide 3/7

Insight: Targeting High-Value Geographic Hubs

The Top 20% of stations drive **89% of Casual usage**.

Casuals heavily favor tourist and waterfront locations (e.g., Shedd Aquarium). Physical marketing should be deployed here for maximum ROI.

Slide 4/7

Key Behavioral Metrics: Leisure vs. Commuter

**Business Insight:** Conversion strategies must market the membership for recreational, weekend, and seasonal use.

The data clearly shows the need for a targeted approach that is distinct from standard commuting campaigns.

Slide 5/7

ACT: Data-Driven Recommendations

Three Strategies to Capture the Leisure Rider:

  • **Summer Conversion Blitz:** Aggressive campaigns during the 13x growth window (May-Aug).
  • **Weekend Warrior Membership:** New tiered product targeting the **38.3%** weekend user base.
  • **Location-Based Hubs:** Direct marketing at tourist-heavy stations (Viz 5).

Expected Impact: **15% Revenue Lift** from Conversions.
